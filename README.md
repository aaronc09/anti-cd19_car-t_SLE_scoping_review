## Overview
This repository contains the full manuscript for a scoping review evaluating Anti-CD19 CAR T-cell therapy as a potential treatment for systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE).

The study synthesizes clinical evidence from case reports and pilot studies to assess remission rates, immunologic outcomes, and safety profiles in patients with refractory SLE.

## Key Findings

- ~97% remission among patients completing follow-up  
- Rapid depletion of autoreactive CD19+ B cells  
- Normalization of C3, C4, and anti-dsDNA  
- Minimal severe adverse effects  
- Evidence of immune system “reset” via naive B-cell repopulation  

---

## Limitations

- Small sample size (n = 73)  
- Case reports and pilot studies  
- Potential publication bias  
- Lack of randomized controlled trials  

---

## Future Directions

- Large-scale randomized clinical trials  
- Long-term safety and durability studies  
- Optimization of allogeneic CAR T-cell approaches  
- Improved accessibility and cost reduction
