# Anti-CD19 CAR T-Cell Therapy for Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE): A Scoping Review

## Overview
This repository contains research materials associated with a scoping review evaluating the potential of Anti-CD19 CAR T-cell therapy for the treatment of systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE).

The study synthesizes clinical evidence from recent case reports and pilot studies to assess remission rates, immunologic outcomes, and safety profiles of CAR T-cell therapy in patients with refractory SLE.

---

## Abstract (Published Version)

> Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE) is a chronic autoimmune disease characterized by autoantibody production leading to tissue and organ damage. Given the central role of B cells in SLE, CAR T-cell therapy targeting CD19 has emerged as a potential treatment strategy. A PRISMA scoping review of 11 studies (73 patients) demonstrated high remission rates, normalization of serologic markers, and no major adverse events in patients completing follow-up. These findings suggest CAR T-cell therapy may be a promising option for refractory SLE, though larger randomized trials are needed.

## Key Findings

- High remission rates observed (~97% among patients completing follow-up)
- Rapid depletion of autoreactive CD19+ B cells
- Normalization of serologic markers (C3, C4, anti-dsDNA)
- Minimal severe adverse effects reported
- Potential for “immune reset” via naive B-cell repopulation

---

## Limitations

- Small sample size (n = 73)
- Reliance on case reports and pilot studies
- Potential publication bias
- Lack of randomized controlled trials

---

## Future Directions

- Large-scale randomized clinical trials
- Long-term safety and remission durability studies
- Optimization of allogeneic CAR T-cell strategies
- Improved accessibility and cost reduction
